The Ripple Network

The underlying technology of XRP is based on the Ripple protocol, a payment protocol that is built on blockchain technology. The Ripple protocol consists of a distributed ledger, which is maintained by a network of independent servers. This network is used to store, verify, and transfer payments. The Ripple protocol is built on a consensus algorithm, which allows it to reach consensus without the need for a trusted third party. It is also designed to be highly efficient, so that transactions can be completed in seconds, rather than minutes or hours.

XRP enables users to send and receive payments of any size, from any currency to any currency, in a matter of seconds. This makes it ideal for international payments, which require the transfer of different currencies. XRP can be used to make payments, exchange currencies, and facilitate transfers of other digital assets. It is currently used by hundreds of companies and financial institutions, including MoneyGram, American Express, and Santander.
